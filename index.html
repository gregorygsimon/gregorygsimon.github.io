Gregory G. Simon

Math, Statistics & Learning
with Python, R (& Julia)

Course Faculty in Statistics at Western Governors University. Previously a Ph.D. student and then Lecturer in Mathematics at the University of Michigan.

My interests are varied, but my current interest is in leveraging machine learning to aid in higher education. At Western Governors University, we have over 100,000 enrolled students and collect detailed educational data on each one. This represents a historically unprecedented access to the data behind student learning. Using Machine Learning, we can detect which practice problems are most effective, alert us when and how a student needs an intervention, and help predict student outcomes based support provided.

My 2016 Ph.D. thesis was a quest for and proof of uniqueness of a canonical lattice inside important algebras in the theory of monstrous moonshine, advised by Dr. Robert L. Griess, Jr. We succeeded in dramatically reducing the complexity of the matrix representation of these algebras.
