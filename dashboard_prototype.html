Student Progress Dashboard Prototype

Python (pandas,matplotlib), data visualization

Business use-case

At WGU, students work asynchronously, proceeding through each course at their own pace. Faculty outreach to struggling students to help them along their way. An outside vendor (MindEdge.com) hosts our online textbook, and accessing this site to get all relevant student data takes 22 mouse-clicks and about 4 minutes (having to click into each quiz result in each Chapter is a big part of this). If a faculty member tries to engage with 20-25 students individually in a work day, it is not currently feasible to check on their progress.

By accessing MindEdge's API, this dashboard reduces the number of clicks to 1 and reduces the time down to about 20 seconds. This empowers the faculty member with the ability to see their student's progress at a glance in all Chapters. So the faculty members can instantly differentiate students who need support vs. who don't, as well as seeing where the student is in the course.

Conclusion

This dashboard prototype has the potential to revolutionize how WGU faculty monitor their students' asychronous progress. It would provide benefit to all stakeholders.

  • On-time Course Completion is the KPI with greatest impetus for improvement. This dashboard can empower faculty to identify and intervene with the students who are at risk of falling behind.
  • Students would receive more relevant and personalized outreach from faculty.
  • Faculty would save time clicking through a slow website interface, and save time by identifying students who do not need any intervention.

Code

The code for this project is available as student_dashboard_prototype.py , which is available below. Some elements were removed from this prototype (like student name, email, ID number, student notes, etc.) to protect students privacy and maintain FERPA compliance. I will highlight a few implementation decisions.

  • The sharey='row' option in plt.subplots() is extremely helpful to visually align the 7 chapters.
  • Similary ax.invert_yaxis() makes the Chapters read downwards from 1 to 7.
  • The colormap cmap=plt.cm.viridis_r is excellent to differentiate hot/current yellow quiz results from colder/old purple quiz results.
  • We reduce opacity and add some small horizontal random jitter to the quiz result circles
    via quiz_df['Percent'].apply(lambda x: x+np.random.normal(0,0.1))
    Otherwise multiple identical scores would be invisible as they would overlap completely.
